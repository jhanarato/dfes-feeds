  <p><strong> The following alert has been issued by the Department of Fire and Emergency Services (DFES).</strong>
  </p>
  <p>
    <br /><span style="color: #777777;">Time of issue: 04:16 PM </span>
    <br /><span style="color: #777777;">Date of issue: 31 December 2022 </span> 
  </p>
  <p><strong>Total Fire Ban affecting</strong>
     
    Pilbara, 
    
    
    
    
    for 1 January 2023
  </p>
  <p>A Total Fire Ban has been declared for 1 January 2023 for the local government districts listed below:
  </p>
  
  
  <p><strong>Pilbara Region:</strong> 	
  </p>
  <ul>
     		
     		
     		
    <li>Karratha - All Day</li> 		
     	
  </ul>
  
  
  
  
  
  
  <p>At these times you must not:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Light, maintain or use a fire in the open air</li>
    <li>Carry out any activity that could start a fire</li>
  </ul>
  <p>This includes:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Open fires for the purpose of cooking, camping or outdoor entertainment are not allowed including fire pits and bonfires.</li>
    <li>Hot work such as metal work, grinding, welding, gas cutting is not allowed in the open air except for business and industry if regulatory conditions are met.</li>
    <li>Off-road activity using a four-wheel drive, quad bike, motorcycle, bobcat or similar vehicle, except for agricultural purposes or by business and industry if regulatory conditions are met.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    <br />If a Harvest and Vehicle Movement Ban has been imposed by your local government, off-road activity for agriculture or business and industry including harvesting is banned. Exceptions are made for essential services.
    <br />
    <br />For a list of activities that can or cannot be carried out during a Total Fire Ban and conditions visit <a href="https://www.dfes.wa.gov.au/totalfirebans" target="_blank"><u>www.dfes.wa.gov.au/totalfirebans</u></a> or by telephoning the Total Fire Ban Information Line on 1800 709 355.
    <br />
    <br />You could be fined up to $25,000 or jailed for 12 months or both if you breach a Total Fire Ban.
    <br />
    <br />Current Total Fire Bans are also available by visiting <a href="www.emergency.wa.gov.au/#totalfirebans" target="_blank"><u>www.emergency.wa.gov.au/#totalfirebans</u></a>
  </p>
